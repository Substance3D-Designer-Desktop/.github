## 🚀 Introduction
Substance 3D Designer for Windows is Adobe's application for designing materials and textures entirely from procedural logic. You work in a node graph where each node performs one operation, and the software resolves the whole network into a finished surface that updates live as you edit. Because the material lives in the graph rather than in a flattened image, it stays resolution-independent and endlessly adjustable — a workflow that suits games, film, and design pipelines where the same surface may be reused at many scales.

## ✨ Highlights

### Graph-Driven Authoring
- **Node graph interface** — Assemble surfaces by connecting nodes, with a real-time 3D preview that reflects changes instantly.
- **Non-destructive edits** — Revisit and retune any node without losing the work that depends on it.
- **Parametric control** — Drive whole families of surface variations from exposed parameters on a single graph.

### Output and Reuse
- **Resolution independence** — Regenerate materials at any output size instead of resampling a fixed bitmap.
- **Pipeline export** — Deliver standard texture maps to renderers, DCC tools, and game engines with ease.

## 🎯 Getting Up and Running
1. Create a fresh substance graph in Substance 3D Designer.
2. Place a generator or noise node to seed your surface.
3. Connect blends and filters to sculpt height, color, and roughness.
4. Read the real-time 3D preview to see the material on geometry.
5. Export your maps to feed your engine or offline renderer.

## ❓ Common Questions
**Is Substance 3D Designer free?**
Substance 3D Designer is a paid Adobe application. A free trial lets you explore the complete node-graph workflow first, and full use is available through Adobe's subscription and perpetual license options. There is no free full copy of the software here, and this project does not provide one or condone bypassing licensing.

**Which versions of Windows are supported?**
The application runs on modern 64-bit Windows, most commonly Windows 10 and Windows 11. Confirm the precise supported baseline against Adobe's requirements for the release you choose to install.

**Is my data secure?**
Your graphs and rendered maps remain on your local Windows storage, and you decide where projects are saved. Since licensing and sign-in go through your Adobe account, protecting those credentials is the key security step for you.
